Project Overview

This research project operates at the cutting-edge intersection of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and Scientific Machine Learning (SciML),, aiming to revolutionize how we predict oceanographic phenomena. Traditional numerical models for forecasting ocean waves and currents are often computationally expensive and time-consuming, limiting their ability to provide real-time insights. Our goal is to overcome these bottlenecks by developing advanced deep learning architectures‚Äîspecifically Nueral Operators (such as MLP, GAN, CNN)‚Äîwhich can learn the hydrodynamic properties.

The selected PhD candidate will focus on designing, training, and validating these neural networks to forecast complex marine dynamics with high fidelity. By integrating fundamental physical laws with data-driven approaches, we aim to achieve simulation speeds orders of magnitude faster than conventional solvers without sacrificing accuracy. This work has profound implications for maritime safety, offshore wind energy planning, and climate monitoring. We are seeking a dedicated researcher eager to bridge the gap between rigorous physical modeling and modern AI, contributing directly to the next generation of real-time ocean forecasting systems.
